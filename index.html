Few Words About Us

For our project “Suburban Soul” we decided to revitalize the land around the Plano Market Square Mall on the intersection of K Avenue and Spring Creek Parkway. To keep the mall aesthetic while adding a more natural and modern touch we decided to go for a half park half outdoor mall renovation of the land.

Sustainability

In line with Plano’s future goals we are taking the initiative of moving our city to a renewable future by using solar energy to reduce our dependence on harmful fossil fuels. Tesla PowerPacks will be charged using 162,000 square feet of roof mounted solar panels and supply clean, renewable energy throughout the park. In addition to this our environmental center will teach visitors about the importance of biodegradable single use plastics, their local ecosystems, and how to reduce their carbon footprint.

Ethics

In an article titled Rejecting Consumerism Can Help The Environment, Wendee Holtcamp discusses her interactions with Compactor ideology and its effects on waste production. Compactor ideology is an extreme form of reducing one’s carbon footprint by vowing to never buy new or at least attempting to buy second hand to reduce the waste and pollutants it takes to create new products. Our space will support the compactor ideology by having services for recycling and up-cycling. This will give support to the community by allowing families to donate items in good condition, but are no longer used, can be materials for new goods or be resold instead of going to a landfill. The last major method of curbing our carbon footprint is controlling how much energy is consumed and maintaining a micro-grid so unused energy is not going to waste. Items such as lights will operate off of solar power. The micro-grid will maintain and manage the total power coming off the grid into and out of the space pulling unused power back into the micro-grid to prevent waste. This will regulate our electric use and guarantee power stored from the solar panels is used before pulling from the Plano power grid.
